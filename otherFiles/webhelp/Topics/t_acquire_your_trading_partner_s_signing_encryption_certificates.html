Acquire Your Trading Partner's Signing/Encryption Certificates

The AS2, AS3, ebMS, OFTPv2, RNIF, and other protocols require use of a digital certificate for encryption and signing purposes. Other security features within VersaLex (e.g., XML Encryption within the Mailbox Packaging tab also require a digital certificate. See Mailbox Packaging Tab . As a prerequisite to setting up a trading relationship, a digital certificate must be acquired from your trading partner. See Certificate Manager for information on digital certificates.

VersaLex provides certificates for some of the pre-configured hosts, but most of the pre-configured hosts will require that you obtain the signing/encryption certificate(s) directly from the trading partner.

The method of certificate exchange must be agreed upon with the trading partner. Certificates may be exchanged through a web site, a courier service, regular mail, or as attachments through electronic mail. (The mailbox Notes tab may come preconfigured with specific information concerning certificate exchange with a particular trading partner.)

Once you have received the trading partner’s certificate(s), you may register the certificate(s) with VersaLex by either saving the file(s) directly in the VersaLex certs\ directory or by importing the certificate(s) . See Importing Certificates

Finally, identify the signing/encryption certificate(s) in the mailbox, for example, see AS2 Mailbox: Certificates Tab. Use the mailbox wizard and follow the easy steps to set the trading partner signing/encryption certificate(s). See Use a Host/Mailbox Wizard.
